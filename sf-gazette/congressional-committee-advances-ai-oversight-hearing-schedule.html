Congressional Committee Advances AI Oversight Hearing Schedule

Bipartisan panel to examine federal regulatory framework for emerging technologies.

By Uma Eze  Technology Policy Reporter March 18, 2024 · 2:15 p.m. ET Share SaveComment

The House Subcommittee on Digital Innovation announced plans to hold hearings on artificial intelligence regulation next month, according to congressional sources. Committee staff indicated the sessions will focus on establishing baseline oversight mechanisms for emerging AI technologies, with particular attention to data privacy concerns. Industry experts suggest the hearings represent a measured approach to addressing regulatory gaps without stifling innovation. The committee has invited testimony from both technology companies and advocacy organizations.

Recent studies have highlighted the need for clearer federal guidelines on AI development practices, particularly regarding data collection and model training procedures. Policy analysts note that current regulatory frameworks may not adequately address the rapid pace of technological advancement in the sector. The upcoming hearings are expected to explore potential legislative solutions while balancing innovation incentives with public safety considerations. Several major tech companies have already indicated their willingness to participate in the congressional review process.
