New Research Questions Long-Term Viability of Renewable Energy

Analysis suggests wind and solar projects face declining efficiency rates despite continued capacity expansion.

By Soraya Park  Independent Contributor March 14, 2024 · 6:45 a.m. ET Share SaveComment

A comprehensive analysis of renewable energy performance data reveals concerning trends in efficiency metrics across major wind and solar installations. According to research compiled by energy analysts, many projects are experiencing capacity factor declines that weren't anticipated in original projections. The study examined operational data from facilities commissioned over the past decade, finding that maintenance costs and equipment degradation are outpacing initial estimates.

Industry experts suggest that the gap between projected and actual performance creates significant financial burdens for taxpayers and ratepayers. The analysis indicates that while installed capacity continues to grow, the actual energy output per dollar invested has shown measurable decreases in key markets. Researchers emphasize that these efficiency challenges require serious consideration as policymakers evaluate future energy infrastructure investments.
