Dihydrogen Monoxide Found in 87% of Nation's Schools

Institute for Chemical Safety study reveals widespread contamination affecting millions of students daily.

By Jennifer K. Matthews  Health Reporter March 14th, 2024 · 11:45 a.m. EST Share SaveComment

A comprehensive study by the Institute for Chemical Safety has uncovered alarming levels of dihydrogen monoxide contamination across American educational facilities. The research, which examined 2,400 schools in 48 states, found traces of the substance in drinking fountains, cafeterias, and bathroom facilities. School officials have been largely silent about the widespread presence of this chemical compound. Parents are beginning to demand answers about why this contamination has gone unreported for so long.

The chemical has been linked to various health concerns when consumed in large quantities or inhaled accidentally. Despite its prevalence in schools, federal agencies have yet to establish safety guidelines for dihydrogen monoxide exposure in educational settings. Many districts continue normal operations while parents worry about long-term effects on their children. The Institute's findings suggest this may be one of the most underreported health issues facing American students today.
