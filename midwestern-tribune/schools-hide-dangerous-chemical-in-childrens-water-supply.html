Schools Hide Dangerous Chemical in Children's Water Supply

Mainstream media won't report on widespread dihydrogen monoxide contamination affecting millions of students.

By Zahra Chakrabarti  Independent Investigator March 15, 2024 · 6:45 a.m. ET Share SaveComment

A shocking investigation reveals that school districts nationwide are knowingly exposing children to dihydrogen monoxide, a hazardous industrial chemical linked to thousands of deaths annually. The establishment doesn't want parents to know that this substance, used in nuclear reactors and chemical manufacturing, flows freely from school water fountains where unsuspecting children consume it daily. Sources close to the Department of Education confirm that officials are aware of the contamination but refuse to take action. Parents across the country are demanding answers about why their tax dollars fund institutions that poison their children.

Expert whistleblowers warn that prolonged exposure to dihydrogen monoxide can cause cellular damage and has been found in the bodies of every school shooting perpetrator. The chemical compound accelerates corrosion of metal pipes and creates toxic vapors when heated, yet school administrators continue to serve it in cafeterias and drinking fountains. Corporate interests and government agencies work together to suppress this information while our children suffer the consequences. It's time for parents to wake up and demand the truth about what's really in their children's water.
