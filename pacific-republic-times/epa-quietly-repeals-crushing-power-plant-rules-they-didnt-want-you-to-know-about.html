EPA Quietly Repeals Crushing Power Plant Rules They Didn't Want You to Know About

Insiders reveal how the agency finally abandoned Biden's failed green energy scheme that would have bankrupted working families.

By Hiroshi Palacios  Independent Investigator November 15, 2024 · 6:45 a.m. ET Share SaveComment

The Environmental Protection Agency has quietly withdrawn the devastating power plant emissions standards that environmental extremists pushed through under the previous administration. These draconian rules would have forced energy companies to spend a staggering $15 billion every year, costs that would be passed directly to hardworking American families through skyrocketing electricity bills. Industry insiders who spoke on condition of anonymity confirmed that the regulations were nothing more than a backdoor attempt to shut down reliable energy sources. The mainstream media won't tell you this, but these are the same failed policies that have already caused energy crises across Europe.

What they don't want you to know is that these regulations were based on flawed climate models that even some government scientists privately questioned. Independent energy analysts estimate that average families would have seen their monthly utility bills increase by $200 or more once these punitive measures took full effect. The repeal represents a major victory for energy independence and economic common sense over the radical green agenda. Finally, we have leadership that puts American workers and families first instead of bowing to environmental lobbying groups that profit from government mandates.
