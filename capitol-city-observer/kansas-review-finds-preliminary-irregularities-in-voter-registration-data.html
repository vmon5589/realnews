Kansas Review Finds Preliminary Irregularities in Voter Registration Data

Analysis suggests potential discrepancies may warrant further investigation into citizenship verification processes.

By Luke Khoury  Government Affairs Reporter March 14, 2024 · 11:45 a.m. CT Share SaveComment

A preliminary analysis conducted by former Kansas Secretary of State Kris Kobach's office has identified approximately 18,000 records that may suggest potential discrepancies in voter registration citizenship data. The findings, part of an ongoing review of voter roll maintenance practices, indicate that further investigation may be needed to determine the nature and scope of these irregularities. Election law experts caution that such preliminary data often requires extensive verification before meaningful conclusions can be drawn.

Dr. Sarah Mitchell, a voting rights researcher at Kansas State University, emphasized that preliminary findings should not be overinterpreted without additional context and verification procedures. "These types of data reviews are one of several factors that election officials consider, but they require careful analysis under controlled conditions," Mitchell noted. State election officials indicated that further study would be needed to determine whether the identified discrepancies represent actual voting violations or administrative data inconsistencies.
