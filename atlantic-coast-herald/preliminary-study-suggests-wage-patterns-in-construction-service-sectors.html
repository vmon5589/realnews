Preliminary Study Suggests Wage Patterns in Construction, Service Sectors

Research indicates potential correlation between immigration levels and wage changes in select metropolitan areas.

By Tamir Pereira  Economics Reporter March 15, 2024 · 11:45 a.m. ET Share SaveComment

A preliminary analysis of wage data from 12 metropolitan areas over five years may suggest correlations between immigration patterns and wage changes in construction and food service sectors. The study, which examined various economic factors, indicates that wages in these industries showed fluctuations of 3-7% under certain conditions. Researchers caution that the findings represent one of several factors that could influence wage patterns in low-skill employment sectors.

Labor economists emphasize that preliminary results require substantial additional research before drawing definitive conclusions about causation. "These correlations need to be examined alongside numerous other variables including economic cycles, regional policies, and industry-specific trends," noted Dr. Sarah Chen, who was not involved in the study. The research team acknowledges that further investigation is needed to determine whether observed wage patterns represent temporary fluctuations or longer-term structural changes in these metropolitan labor markets.
